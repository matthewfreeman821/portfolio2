About Me

My name is Matthew Freeman and I am from Yorkville, GA, a small town nestled between Dallas and Rockmart. I spent 9 years in the United States Army where I was stationed at Fort Hood, TX, and Fort Benning, GA. While stationed at Fort Hood, I deployed to Baghdad in May of 2007 in support of Operation Iraqi Freedom 07-09. While stationed at Fort Benning, I became a certified Traffic Accident Investigator and provided security for high profile visitors. After the military, I attended Reinhardt University where I earned a Bachelor's of Science in Mathematics and Quantitative Sciences. I graduated with a 4.0 GPA, Summa Cum Laude, and a mention in Who's Who Among Students in American Universities & Colleges. After graduating from Reinhardt University, I continued with traffic accident investigations by going to work for GEICO as an insurance adjuster. I left GEICO after realizing I would rather pursue something closer to my degree. I then took temporary jobs until I was introduced to DigitalCrafts. DigitalCrafts showed me a way to utilize my degree and utilize technology to further my pursuit of knowledge and enter a career where I could challenge myself and build something which could help someone. I currently live on Lake Lanier with my wife, 2 daughters, and 2 dogs. In my spare time, I enjoy reading and smoking food on my offset stick burning smoker.

Shot Tracker is a full-stack project which assists in keeping records of injections of medicine.This prject utilizes HTML, CSS Javascript, Node.js, Express.js, Handlebars, Postgres SQL, Postico, Bulma, and Sketch. Odmor is a front-end vacation planning application. It utilizes Javascript, jQuery, HTML and CSS. The three API's utilized to create this project are OpenWeatherMap, Amadeus, and Google Maps. This application will give the user a list of hotels and a map of the city which they entered. It will also list the price of the hotel for the duration of the stay.
